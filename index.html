From AI to AO: Predicting How Points End in Tennis

Predicting How Points End in Tennis was a competition hosted on CrowdAnalytix. Since this was my first data science competition, I spent a lot of time learning and exploring different algorithms to get a feel about how each algorithm worked. The goal of the compeittion was to predict how the point ended: winner, unforced error, or forced error. The model I settled on was XGBoost, which is the go-to algorithm among Kagglers.

Reinforcement Learning- Blackjack

I am a huge fan of Ed Thorp. So it shouldn't surprise you that I enjoy games that require a blend of skill and luck: blackjack, poker, trading, etc... After spending a good portion of my summer (during the commute to my internship) practicing counting cards and various blackjack strategies, I wondered if a machine could learn to play blackjack optimally. Although I did not discover blackjack's holy grail, I did learn a lot about Markov Decision Processes (MDPs), Q-learning, and approximating the Q-value using neural networks.
